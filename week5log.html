Project: Make a Desk Organizer

This week, our project was to make a desk organizer. The organizer needed an outlined circle and a minimum of 3 slots for our personal objects and text

How the Shaper works

The shaper is an example of subtractive design, chipping away at a base until the desired desing is reached. Although it is not quite the normal CNC Router, as it is a handheld device that requires the user to move it along the lines of a cut. It is still very much computer assisted design as it automatically adjusts and orients itself to cut exactly the dimmensions of the file and only requires the user to hover over the general area.

Designing in Fusion360

The first part of this process would be to design the shapes we will be carving out of the wood and the specific dimensions of the objects we are working with. We used a sketch in Fusion360.

1. This box represents the wood plank that the organizer will be cut out of.

2. This rounded rectangle is the actual desk organizer.

3. The pot holder with a 3 inch diameter

4. The holder for the box that I made in my laser cutting assignment.

5. a place to put pens and pencils

6. an area for my earphones case.

7. now that the sketch is complete, we can extrude parts of it to get an image of what the desk organizer will look like

8. I will extrude by 3/4 inches as that is the depth of the wood

9. for the pocket cuts, I extrude by only half an inch.

10. for the pot holder, since that is an exterior cut, part of it should be partially extruded.

First Attempt: failure

When moving the file from fusion to photoshop, there were settings on how to the file should be opened in illustrator. I thought those settings would not matter, but it turns out that prompt was asking how to scale the image.While doing the cut, I kept wondering if the dimensions were smaller than they should have been, I kept going nonetheless. I stopped early after cutting out the pocket for the earphones case that I had on me, checked and saw that it was definetly too small.This was how far I got with that attempt at the organizer. eveything was way too small to even pretend that it was intended. I didn't bother cutting out the pot or cutting the organizer out of the base because it was clear that I would have to start over.
