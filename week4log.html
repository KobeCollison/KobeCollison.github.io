Week 4: Digital Embroidery

snitches get stitches

Theme, the lunar swan

The theme for this logo was the moon, I wanted something that revolved around the moon shining out of a black background. The end result was the lunar swan.

The Hardest part, the idea

The theme for this logo is simple, the moon.In the beggining, the only thing I had for the icon was a moon based theme. I latched on to the moon idea instantly and looked to find some second thing to mix with that to make a logo, but nothing was working like I would have wanted.

Sketch Attempts

I tried combining the moon with a star, that was boring and lame. I tried combining the moon and a sunflower to call the business moonflower, but things were always either weird or outside of my artistic ability. I then decided to use an animal, after a few animals, the one I chose was a swan.

Taking it to illustrator

Once I actually had a concrete idea in my head, translating it to illustrator was really easy. I had taken a visual design class in high school where we used illustrator sometimes, but mostly photoshop, which is stil really similar.

for the moon, I created two elipses, the first is just a wite circle for the moon itself, the second is a black ellipse to block the rest of the moon to give it the cresent shape

the swan was created using the pen tool, I used my final sketch as a refrence and drew a swan sillouhette with a white fill over the dark area of the moon.

the rest were just small improvements I made. I used the pen tool to make one of the stars then copy/pasted them at different sizes. and the black part at the intersection between the swan and the moon was to make the difference between the 2 shapes aparent, since they are both no stroke and the same fill, they would have blended together.

Framing and text

The framing and text additions went smoothly as well, it might be bending the rules of the assignment a little bit but I think it still looks good.

Since my logo was almost entirely curves, I felt that the framing I would do for the text should be curve based as well. I used the pen tool to add this flourish to the design to make way for text.

I tried out a few fonts from online, in the end, I fell in love with this "Black Magic" Font. I will probably use it for more things in the future.

The Black Magic font worked really well with the logo, The one thing I still needed that I hand forgot to think about until now was what kind of business this was, as that will be a part of the design.

In the end, I decided it would be a catering business. I'm not entirely sure why I chose That I guess it was just at the top of my mind, and in hindsight, I probably would have preferred this to be some sort of restaurant (close enough I guess.) It was then that I thought to myself That I could have a very small bag for the sake of holding cookies.

Now make it into a bag

Right away!
