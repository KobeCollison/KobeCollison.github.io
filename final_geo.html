Two Finals in One

For my advanced geometry class, our final project is to imagine a world that applies by a different geometry to ours. We would also have to fabricate a model of some aspect or artifact of the world. Because there was so much overlap with this and my digifab final. The professors allowed me do both projects in one, A model of a world with a unique geometry, with a model fabricated using MULTIPLE digital fabrication methods.

My world

The world I thought of is very similar to ours. It is still planet Earth and the geometry is the exact same. However, technology is sufficiently advanced and it has allowed for all sorts of new inventions. The most impressive new technology being Geometric Spatial Gluing, or GSG for short. GSG is a technology that has allowed for the bending of the rules of space. connecting two separate areas in space and linking them in a way that approaching and travelling through one end of the linked space will result in said object exiting the other linked space.

GSG technology has revolutionized infastructure, redifining our idea of what a building could be. saved transportation, trivializing many of the traffic concerns of the past that were getting worse and worse as the population incereases (imagine an intersection where there are portals that connecting the opposite side of the road in each direction. ) GSG tech has even been leading to major medical advancements recently.

The Hedron is a new ride at the amusement park, (name). The hedron offers the excitemnt of a full rollercoaster in a fraction of the space. Along with the mind-bending of the ride thanks to the GSG technology powering it. The Hedron is encased in a box of GSG plating. each side of the cube being glued to another side. For example, if side A and side B are glued together, a rollercoaster cart would drive into side A only to reappear at side B, continuing the ride.

For my Geometry final project, I have decided to use the Hedron as an artifact that I will be making a model of along with my partners Bret and Nick. The process for creating the model will be covered in the next section.

Taking it to illustrator

Once I actually had a concrete idea in my head, translating it to illustrator was really easy. I had taken a visual design class in high school where we used illustrator sometimes, but mostly photoshop, which is stil really similar.

for the moon, I created two elipses, the first is just a wite circle for the moon itself, the second is a black ellipse to block the rest of the moon to give it the cresent shape

the swan was created using the pen tool, I used my final sketch as a refrence and drew a swan sillouhette with a white fill over the dark area of the moon.

the rest were just small improvements I made. I used the pen tool to make one of the stars then copy/pasted them at different sizes. and the black part at the intersection between the swan and the moon was to make the difference between the 2 shapes aparent, since they are both no stroke and the same fill, they would have blended together.
